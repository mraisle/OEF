Nov 23, 2021  | 
By Gareth McGrath Verified
 |  Gaston Gazette Verified
Roughly 25 miles north of Wilmington in rural Pender County sits Moores Creek National Battlefield. The National Park site is famous as the home of one of the first Patriot victories of the Revolutionary War and the last broadsword charge by Scottish Highlanders, which failed miserably in a world of muskets. But recently the site, one of only two national battlefields in North Carolina, has been dealing with a more insidious foe — repeated flooding events.
