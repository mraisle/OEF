Hurricane Florence’s floodwaters carried E. coli, other bacteria across Eastern NC

Sep 01, 2021  | 
By Adam Wagner Verified
 |  News & Observer Verified
Scientists found several species of bacteria in floodwaters left behind by September 2018’s Hurricane Florence, according to a new study from researchers at N.C. State University and UNC-Chapel Hill. Scientists took samples within two weeks of the storm, and again in mid-October, about a month after the storm. Every sample had E. coli in it, and scientists found Arcobacter butzleri — a relatively recently discovered bacteria that can cause diarrhea in humans — in 65 of the 88 samples.
